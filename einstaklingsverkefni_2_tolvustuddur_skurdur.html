Project 2 - Computer aided cuts

This project is all about using cutters, a laser cutter and a vinyl cutter in this case, to create the shapes that you desire.

Before the project is designed the prerequisite is to perform a self-made kerf test

Project requirements

The project requirements is in 2 parts (in Icelandic are the main design ones, since that's the way I got them and the others are ones I wrote from other places in the project description):

  • Notaðu vínylskerann til að búa til eitthvað. 100x50cm skurðarflötur max.
  • Hannaðu parametrískt, geirneglt (pressfit) módel af byggingareiningum. Módelið þarf að vera skalanlegt á mismunandi máta (efnisþykkt, hæðir og breiddir sem nemandi velur), útfært með parametríum í hugbúnaði að eigin vali. Mælt er með Autodesk Fusion 360 ef nemandi vill fá aðstoð kennara, en hafi nemandi tök á því að nota t.d. Solidworks eða Rhino 3D + Grasshopper þá er það í lagi.
  • Use a laser cutter to cut out your design of a parametric model and build your design.
  • Document the entire process of this project on your website
  • Perform the prerequisite kerf test

    Part 0 - The idea and design requirements

    Before we start designing (or maybe some would say this is the first step of designing) we must come with an idea and some design requirements for that idea. Now it just so happens that I am working on an electric cargo ship and for that project we need to maintain some batteries and we need to learn to use solar panels.
    So we have our idea, we are going to make a solar charging station for the batteries to be used in the proof of concept boat.

    To make this wee ned something that can:

    • Hold 5 solar panels (with options to add/remove solar panels)
    • Hold at least 1 battery which could be any of the sizes and types that I currently own.
    • Have room for connecting wires between the solar panels and the batteries
    • Have over voltage protection for the batteries to protect them from damage.
    • Have under voltage protection for the batteries to protect them from damage, in case the voltage from the panels becomes too low.
    • Be able to adjust the angle of the solar panels
    • Require less than or the same as the available material.
